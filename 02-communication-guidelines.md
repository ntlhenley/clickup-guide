# Communication Guidelines

## Slack Dos and Don'ts

### Dos:
- **Do communicate clearly.** Use concise language and ask clarifying questions if needed.
- **Do respect the availability of others.** Acknowledge their time and respond during working hours when possible.
- **Do use threads for discussions.** This keeps channels organized and prevents off-topic conversations from clogging the main chat.
- **Do utilize emojis or GIFs appropriately.** They can add context and a friendly tone to your messages but shouldn't replace clear communication.

### Don'ts:
- **Don't spam.** Avoid sending multiple messages when one message could suffice.
- **Don't disregard the channel purpose.** Each channel has a specific focus, keep discussions relevant to that topic.
- **Don't hesitate to ask for help.** If you're unsure about something, reach out rather than guessing.

## HeyOrca Exception
HeyOrca is a tool designed for asynchronous communication on marketing tasks. In this context, team members should:
- Use HeyOrca for feedback on visual assets rather than in real-time chat.
- Be specific in your comments, referencing particular elements when reviewing content.
- Allow 24 hours for response time to support a healthy workflow.

## Guidelines for Task-Based Communication Across Platforms
- **Clarity is key.** Regardless of the platform, always ensure that your tasks are clearly defined with objectives, deadlines, and accountability.
- **Use project management tools.** For task assignments, utilize tools like ClickUp to streamline processes, ensuring everyone is on the same page.
- **Follow up regularly.** Check in on tasks through scheduled updates to keep everyone accountable and on track.
- **Be adaptable.** Different platforms may require different communication styles; adjust as necessary based on the group and task dynamics.

By following these communication guidelines, the team can maintain a collaborative and productive environment across all platforms.
